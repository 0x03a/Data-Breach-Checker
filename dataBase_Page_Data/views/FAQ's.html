Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our Data Breach Checker service

What is a Data Breach Checker?

Our Data Breach Checker is a security tool that helps you verify if your passwords have been exposed in known data breaches. It checks your password against a database of compromised credentials to help you maintain better security.

How does the password checking work?

When you enter a password, our system securely checks it against a database of known compromised passwords. We use advanced hashing techniques to ensure your password is never stored in plain text. If your password is found in our database, it means it has been exposed in previous data breaches.

Is it safe to check my passwords?

Yes, it's completely safe! We use industry-standard security practices. Your passwords are never stored, and we use secure hashing to check them. The check is done in real-time, and we don't keep any record of the passwords you check.

What should I do if my password is breached?

If your password is found in our database, you should immediately change it to a stronger, unique password. We recommend using a combination of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Also, make sure to use different passwords for different accounts.

How often should I check my passwords?

We recommend checking your passwords whenever you create a new account or change an existing password. It's also good practice to periodically check your passwords, especially for important accounts like email, banking, or social media.

What makes a strong password?

A strong password should be at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. Avoid using personal information, common words, or sequential patterns. Consider using a password manager to generate and store strong, unique passwords.
